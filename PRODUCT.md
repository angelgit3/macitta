# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Macitta serves learners in public universities in Latin America, especially students who need practical English vocabulary to read documentation, study technical material, and prepare for exams. They use the app in short daily sessions, often on mobile, sometimes with unreliable connectivity.

## Product Purpose

Macitta is an offline-first spaced repetition platform for vocabulary learning. Success means a learner can open the app, know exactly what to study next, complete a focused session, and trust that progress is saved locally and synced when possible.

## Brand Personality

Focused, precise, and quietly aspirational. The interface should feel like a well-made study instrument: calm enough for reading, polished enough to earn trust, and warm enough to feel human.

## Anti-references

Avoid neon SaaS dashboards, generic blue-purple gradients, heavy glassmorphism, playful gamification overload, and green as the brand accent. Avoid interfaces that feel like crypto, gaming, or decorative productivity templates.

## Design Principles

1. Put the study task first: every screen should make the next useful action obvious.
2. Make reading comfortable: long passages, questions, answers, and review explanations need generous contrast and rhythm.
3. Keep one connected system: dashboard, decks, study, TOEFL, auth, and profile should share the same component vocabulary.
4. Use color with restraint: indigo-black carries the workspace, periwinkle marks primary action, amber marks TOEFL/listening emphasis, and sage is reserved for success.
5. Preserve reliability: offline status, loading, errors, and sync feedback must be clear without stealing attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and UI controls. Support keyboard focus, reduced motion, 44px minimum touch targets, readable mobile text, and status messaging that does not rely on color alone.
